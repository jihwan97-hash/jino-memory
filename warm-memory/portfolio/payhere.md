# Payhere - Portfolio Research

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-30

## Company Overview
Cloud-based mobile POS system for Korean SMEs
- **Founded:** 2018
- **CEO:** Park Jun-gi
- **Product:** Payhere Terminal (cloud POS app)

## Key Metrics

### Merchants
- **60,000+ merchants** (as of Oct 2024, Forbes Korea)
- Previous reports: 82,000+ (marketing materials)
- Possible consolidation/churn, or data cleanup

### Funding History
- **Series A (Dec 2021):** $84.2M (100억원)
  - Lead: DST Global, Vision Plus Capital
- **Series B (Apr 2023):** 20억원
- **Total raised:** ~120억원+ cumulative

## Product Features
- **Zero fees:** No monthly fees, transaction fees, or penalties
- **Cloud-based:** Tablets/smartphones (no physical hardware)
- **Integrations:**
  - Naver reservations
  - Real-time inventory management
  - Multi-location management (franchises)
  - Excel data import/export

### Recent Product Additions (2025)
- **Tax refund service** for foreign tourists
- **Pop-up store short-term rental** model
- Focus on tourism-heavy regions (Seoul, Jeju, Busan, Gyeongju)

## Market Position
- **#1 cloud POS in Korea**
- Strong in: cafes, restaurants, retail, salons, nail shops
- Expanding to: franchise chains, pop-up stores

## Competitive Landscape
- Traditional POS: Legacy hardware vendors (expensive, monthly fees)
- Cloud competitors: Smaller players, but Payhere has brand leadership
- Fintech integration: Naver Pay, Kakao Pay (partnerships not competitors)

## Status Assessment
**🟢 GROWTH STAGE - MARKET LEADER**

Active customer acquisition, especially in tourism vertical. Merchant count consolidation (82K→60K) is normal for SaaS (churn + data cleanup). Fundamentals remain strong.

## Key Metrics to Watch
- Merchant retention rate (60-month cohort analysis)
- Revenue per merchant (ARPU)
- International tourist transaction volume (2026 recovery)
- Franchise adoption rate

## Investment Implications
- **Market position:** Clear #1 in cloud POS Korea
- **Business model:** Sustainable (transaction fees, not subscriptions)
- **Growth vectors:** Tourism recovery + franchise expansion
- **Risk:** Korean SME market saturation, fintech competition

## Catalysts for 2026
- Foreign tourist volume recovery (visa-free travel expansion)
- Franchise chain standardization push
- Potential Series C for international expansion?
